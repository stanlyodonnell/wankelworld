Citroen GS Birotor

After Citroën experimented with wankel powered M35. They thought The Citroën GS Birotor was unfortunately launched at the 1973 Frankfurt Auto show, when the oil crisis was in full swing.  A total of 865 Citroen GS (also know as GZ) Birotors where built in the period from 1974 to 1975. With 834 built in 1974 and 31 built during 1975.

Citroën GS Birotor
 
 

Even though there was high hopes for the Citroen Birotor. The new wankel engine from Comotor wasn't with out its problems. Like most wankel rotary engines of the day it suffered from high fuel consumption. But there was also problems with gas tightness around the seal excessive apex seal wear, high emission levels and high oil consumption. This also lead to a lack of reliability which in turn lead to Citroen making the decision to recall all the GS Birotors. But luckily not all the Birotors where returned to Citroen. A few managed to escape the crusher including a right hand drive version which was last I heard in Australia below there are a couple of photos of this right hand drive version.  The Birotor was front engine front wheel drive and mated to the KKM 624 comtor wankel was a 3 speed semi automatics transmission. Apart from the new wankel rotary engine fitted to the Citroen Birotor it also had another interesting technical advancement in the form of Hydropneumatic self leveling suspension system.

The KKM 624 twin rotor wankel engine produced 107 Hp at 6500rpm and covered the quarter mile in 18.6 sec this was pretty good for a luxury car of the day. If the Citroen GS (GZ) Birotor worked out, Citroen had plans to produce a 3 rotor wankel engine which they where going to fit to their CX model. But their financial difficulties put a stop to this project, what a shame that is, I for one would have loved to see another triple rotor wankel vehicle on the road. But alas the Mazda Cosmo 20B is the only triple rotor production car to dated.
