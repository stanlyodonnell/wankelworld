Mazda R100 / Familia Rotary

Rotary power for the masses

Engine 10A
Power 100 hp
Body Styles Coupe / Sedan
First Export USA

Overview

The Mazda R100 brought the rotary engine to the masses. Based on the Familia compact platform, the R100 proved that rotary power could work in an affordable, everyday car. It was Mazda's first rotary export to the United States, introducing American drivers to the unique character of the Wankel engine.

Available as both a 2-door coupe and 4-door sedan, the R100 offered the smooth, high-revving performance of the 10A rotary in a practical, compact package. It established the formula that Mazda would refine throughout the rotary era: innovative engine technology in accessible, driver-focused cars.

Specifications

Engine 10A (982cc twin-rotor)
Power 100 hp @ 7,000 rpm
Torque 135 Nm @ 3,500 rpm
Transmission 4-speed manual
Body Styles 2-door coupe, 4-door sedan
Weight 880 kg
0-60 mph 10.8 seconds

Legacy

The R100 was crucial in establishing Mazda's international reputation. Its success in export markets proved there was global demand for rotary-powered cars. Today, R100s are rare and sought-after by collectors, representing the moment when the rotary engine transitioned from exotic curiosity to practical powerplant.
