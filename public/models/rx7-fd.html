Overview

Arguably the finest rotary sports car ever made. The FD RX-7 is a masterpiece of design and engineering, with sequential twin-turbocharging delivering the broadest power band of any rotary engine.

Designed by Yoichi Sato, the FD's timeless styling looks as fresh today as it did in 1992. Its 50/50 weight distribution, compact dimensions, and high-revving rotary engine make it one of the greatest Japanese sports cars ever created.

Sequential Twin-Turbo System

The FD's 13B-REW features a sophisticated sequential twin-turbo system. The primary turbo spools at low RPM for instant response, while the secondary turbo engages at higher RPM for maximum power. This eliminates turbo lag while delivering strong power throughout the entire rev range.

The system uses a complex network of vacuum lines, solenoids, and actuators to manage boost pressure. While this can be maintenance-intensive, the result is one of the most responsive turbocharged engines ever made.

Model Years & Updates

Series 6 (1992-1995)

The original FD featured a 255 hp engine, 16-inch wheels, and a distinctive front end with pop-up headlights. The interior was driver-focused with a simple, functional layout.

Series 7 (1996-1998)

Power increased to 276 hp thanks to improved engine management and a larger intercooler. Restyled front and rear bumpers, 17-inch wheels, and interior updates.

Series 8 (1999-2002)

Final iteration with refined suspension, improved aero, and the Spirit R special edition. The Spirit R featured BBS wheels, Recaro seats, and Bilstein suspension.

Racing Heritage

The FD competed successfully in various motorsport series, including the IMSA GTU and GTO classes. The 24 Hours of Le Mans saw RX-7s compete in the GT2 class. The car's balanced handling and reliable rotary engine made it a favorite among privateer racers.

Legacy

The FD is now a certified classic, with values rising steadily. Its timeless design, incredible handling, and unique rotary character make it one of the most sought-after Japanese sports cars. Clean, stock examples command premium prices, while modified cars offer endless tuning potential.
