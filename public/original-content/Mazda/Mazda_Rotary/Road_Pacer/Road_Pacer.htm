Mazda Roadpacer

        The Mazda Road Pacer is an unusual car, it is a cross between a Australian GM (Holden)  4 door 5 seater Kingswood HX Sedan and a typical Japanese luxury car of the time, with one uncommon extra the Mazda 13B Rotary. Mazda purchased Holden Kingswood body shells from GM Australia and out fitted them with a luxurious interior (some had very tacky interiors) and a Mazda 13B Rotary engine, this was the best bit of the whole car in my opinion.

In March of 1975 the body parts and the machine part were purchased from the Australian GM- Holden corporation, and where fitted with the Mazda 13B Rotary engine the " Roadpacer" was then sold with in the Japanese market, as a luxury car.

A photo of the rare Mazda Roadpacer. Front view of the Roadpacer A rear view of the  Mazda Roadpacer

Mazda developed this unusual car, with a minimum of fuss and with very little plant investment, as most of the parts required for this car where either already being produced by Mazda or Mazda purchased the part from Holden Australia. This enabled a very short development time on this project. 

The Mazda Roadpacer was produced between 1975 and 1977 with only approximately 840 making it to market. And Most of those Mazda Roadpacers where purchased by Government department as a Limo (or town car) for high ranking officials. The Roadpacer was aimed at the  government, company  high end of the personal car buyers as a luxurious car with high quality, and high-class.  Click here to see my copy of Road Pacer sales Catalogue, I purchased this in while in Japan.

 

This interior is on of the better ones. As you can see the engine bay of the Road Pacer doesn't look much different from any other Rotary engine bay of the time, with the exception that the Road Pacer has a little more room.

 Although the Mazda Roadpacer's 13B wankel rotary engine produced 135 hp ( this is more the the Holden HX 6 cylinder engine) it  was still a little slow off the line as the car was a little bulky at 1575kg and the 13B only produced 138Nm of torque. The performance of the Roadpacer was fairly poor, as you could well imagine with such a heavy car.   But once the Roadpacer was up and running it was a very nice ride and the performance was a little better with a top speed of 166km/h. It has been reported the the fuel consumption could be as high as 26lt per 100kms, but this seems very high even for a wankel. I have heard that there is Mazda Roadpacer in a museum in Perth, Western Australia. 

One thing I love about the Mazda Roadpacer is all the optional gadgets that one could get with the Mazda Roadpacer. Some of these include a stereo that was controlled from either the front seats or from the rear (those government officials can be very controlling), a dictation system for the hard working official. For the safety conscious the central locking would automatically lock when the car reached 10km/h. And to make sure that the chauffeur was not speeding a chime would sound once 90km/h was reached.
