Citroen M35

Like many of the car manufacturers of the day Citroën thought that the wankel engine was the way of the future. So they jumped on the band wagon a produced the M35 prototype. Initially Citroen planed to produce 500 M35 prototypes. But production was going a little slow, so Citroën started to skip serial numbers to give the appearance that 500 cars where being built. In the end there where only 267 M35's produced. The cars where maintained by specialist from the research department of Citroën.

Citroën group photo with the pride of their fleet at the time  the M35
 

The M35 body is built by Heuliez at Cerizay. The first six bodies where built in the autumn of 1969. But the project itself didn't officially start until January 1970. The first six bodies where painted in colors that differ from the main production run. the first 6 M35's where delivered to customers at the end of 1969, an additional 212 where delivered during 1970 and the final 49 where delivered in 1971.

Na interesting fact is that all M 35 prototypes a written on the front guards "prototype Citroën M35 nº??"

The first of these cars where driven by professionals for over 1 million km over 5 years. These cars have much better performance then later "production" cars. Some of these first where been seen by eyewitnesses reportedly doing over 175km/h, there main production cars had a top speed of 144km/h. The cars will be maintained by specialist from the research department. All problems have to be reported directly to this department.

The Citroën M35 was loosely based a the Citroën Ami 8 body, and for those of you who are lucky enough to own a Citroën M35 here are some of differences and similarities between the Citroën M35 and the Citroën Ami 8. 

Nose cone is a completely different shape compared to the Ami 8.
Headlights and indicators are identical to Ami 8 ones.
Grille is a black one as found on the early Ami 8 but it has a special logo in the centre. 
Bonnet has different locks compared to the Ami 8.
Front wings are identical to Ami 8 ones.
Windscreen is unique to the M35.
Front doors are longer then normal Ami 8 ones.
Rear wings are shorter then those on an Ami 8 service because on the M35 the doors are longer.
Boot lid is not the same as the Ami 8 Berline.
The chassis is specially designed for the M35.
Suspension is specially made for the hydraulic suspension.

To make the car more desirable  to potential buyer Citroën offered and unlimited warranty on all M35's, but due  the cars suffering from poor engine performance, reliability problems warranty costs where very high. In 1974 Citroen cancelled the warranties on all M35's. Citroën gave M35 owners the option of either keeping their cars or returning them to Citroën. Of course those who chose to return their cars where given a generous financial agreements when buying another Citroën. All cars given back to Citroën where destroyed. Quite a few customers chose to keep their M35's, but they had to sign contracts in which Citroën does not have to keep spares parts. Most M35's don't keep running for a very long, most having engine or ignition problems and are either scrapped or put into storage by the owners. It is beleived that there are only about sixty Citroën M35's remaining today.
