Mazda wins the 1991 Le Mans 24 Hour endurance Race

 

The Mazda 787B was the outright winner of the 1991 24 Hours of Le Mans  on June 22nd and 23rd, 1991, at the Sarthe circuit, Le Mans, France. Mazda is the only Japanese manufacture to win Le Mans to date, not only that, they are the only manufacture to win this legendary race with the MIGHTY ROTARY ENGINE. The winning pilots (I say pilots because driving this race car would be like flying a fighter jet) were Johnny Herbert, Bertrand Gachot, and Volker Weidler, they drove 362 laps and 4923.2 km, and finished an astonishing 27.2 km ahead of the second place car. This driving distance corresponds to how much a normal passenger car would be used over 3 to 6 months, under normal circumstances. The average speed was 205.3 kph. They lead from lap 320 to the end (the last three hours).  Of course, this victory was not just the result of the cars, excellent drivers, it also could not have been achieved without a support staff totaling 120 people from 10 different countries.

In 1990, FISA decided that starting in 1992 the rotary engines would not be allowed( I guess that they couldn't stand a rotary powered car winning by so much), but this changed couple years later.       

The R26B engine that won was normally aspirated and had four rotors and three sequential spark plugs per rotor. The capacity is 2622 cc actual, rated by FIA formula at 4708 cc. It produced an 700 bhp at 9000 rpm and 62 mkg torque at 6500 rpm (448 lb-ft). It was electronically Fuel injected and consumption was 51.881 liters per 100 km at an average speed of 213.58 kph. The air intakes had continuously variable geometry controlled by the engine electronics. The transmission had 5 speeds  from a Porsche design gearbox and a limited slip differential.

The Mazda 787B car weighed 830 kg. The chassis was designed by Nigel Stroud and the body was carbon fiber. Wheelbase was 2.66 m by 1.53/1.50 m and overall size 4.78 by 1.99 m. It had 18 in wheels, ventilated carbon fibre brakes. Tires were Dunlop 300-640x18 front and 355-710x18 rear.
