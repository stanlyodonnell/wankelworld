TAG Croco Amphibious Vehicle

Also known as the Rhino

The TAG Croco in action - traversing water with ease

Overview

The Croco is an articulated amphibious vehicle produced by TAG of Switzerland in 1983. The TAG group was hoping that the military or oil and gas exploration companies would be interested in the Croco as it is small enough that it can be transported into remote locations via helicopters.

Unique Articulated Design

A little more on the articulated section of the Croco: It is split into 2 sections (tubs) - the front and back tubs. Although attached, they can rotate independently of each other in the vertical plane. Via a large roller bearing in the centre of the 2 sections, this means that one of the front wheels can go over something like a rock or log, while the rear wheel on the opposite side can go over another large object at the same time (very cool!). This is a truly amazing ability for a reasonably small vehicle.

Impressive Capabilities

Another amazing ability of this little Wankel-powered vehicle is that it has no problem climbing a slope of 45 degrees, or even a sideways slope of 40 degrees. All while carrying a payload of 500kg or 6 people.

Specifications

Manufacturer TAG Group SA (Switzerland)
Year 1983
Also Known As The Rhino
Configuration 2-section articulated (front and rear tubs)
Drive Constant 4x4
Max Slope (Forward) 45 degrees
Max Slope (Sideways) 40 degrees
Payload Capacity 500 kg or 6 people
Engine Wankel Rotary

Current Status

The huge wheels and tyres on the Croco are constant 4x4 which help to avoid axle wind up. The rights to the vehicle are now with Kolper near Karlsruhe, Germany.

Quick Facts

  • Can be transported by helicopter to remote locations
  • Articulated design allows independent vertical rotation of front and rear sections
  • Capable of traversing extreme terrain both on land and water
  • Designed for military and oil/gas exploration applications
