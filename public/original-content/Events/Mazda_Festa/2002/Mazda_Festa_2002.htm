Mazda Festa 2002 was held at Tsukuba Circuit, which is about 3 hours by train (well 4 trains and a taxi) from Tokyo, on September 28th, 2002. I don't know to much about the history of this event, but I do know that it was started in 1989 and has been run at the same location for the past three years.

What can I say but WOW!!!!! what a great event this was! I got to see not one but two of Mazda's famous Le Mans race cars live and in the flesh! This would have to be the best event I have been to so far. Yes even better then the 35th Tokyo Motor Show where I saw the RX-8, for the first time.

Why was this the best event, I hear you ask? Well just being near the Mazda 787B #55 car and the 767B #202 car would have been enough to make this event the best, but it went far beyond that. Not only could you see the car, but you were able to go right up, touch and lean on the cars! That's right, there was no barrier at all! As you can see from the pictures and video, the Mazda staff didn't care that everyone was all over the cars the whole day long. 

Not even when the 767B car was fired up for the display lap did people move back. In fact I was only about 4 or 5 feet from the back of the car. This enabled me to get some great close up shots of the car and also of the Mazda mechanics working on it. 

To get to Tsukuba Circuit is quite an adventure from our nearest train station (Higashi Funabashi) we had to catch 4 trains and 1 taxi, it took us approximately 3 hours to get there and I believe that it would take about 3.5 hours for Tokyo it self.  Travel cost to this event was a little expensive at ¥4000 round trip, but it was well worth the long and expansive trip. I will do it again given the chance.

The weather on the day was far from ideal but that didn't stop us or a lot of other people from going. It was raining on and off for most of the day, but luckily it stoped raining  for a couple of hours in the middle of the day which aloud the track to dry out enough for the Mazda Le Mans 767B car #202 to come out for a display lap.

So what was at this Mazda Festa (Click here for the full event schedule), well one of the first things I saw when I walked through the ticket gate was a huge line up of RX-7's ranging from some very tidy looking RX-7 FC3S up to the very latest (current model) Mazda RX-7 FD3S. All these cars where lined up ready for the "RX-7 Rotary Sports Race". This race was 25 minutes long and kicked off at its scheduled time of 10:15. Even though it was only a short race I found it very entertaining. But unfortunately the bad weather slowed everyone down this combined with a fairly short straight ment the the top speed for the day was only 92.6 km/hr. This top speed was recorded by the winner of the "Rotary Sports Race" Shingo Ikedo in car #11. Please scroll down to see video footage of this race.
