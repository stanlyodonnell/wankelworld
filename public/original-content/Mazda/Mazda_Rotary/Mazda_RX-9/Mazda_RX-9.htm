Mazda RX-9

The Rotary which was never to be

 
The Mazda RX-9 was never released to the public. This is the 78 - 79 version.

Wait before you start sending me emails saying "are you sure there was an RX-9", let me tell you the story of how I came across little known Mazda Rotary. One day a few years back I was looking though a second hand book shop and I found this book with a yellowy-green cover, on the cover was written the words "Mazda RX-9" and being a rotorhead this immediately got my attention. I was excited and confused "what the hell!, there's no such thing as an RX-9". So I brought the book and took it home, I must have looked though it a hundred times, but as there where no pictures of the car, only wiring diagrams I could not work out what it was, as I am a busy person I put it on the book shelf and forgot all about it. Until one day a couple years ago, when I was having a clean out I found it again and said "that's it I have to find out what car this is!". After about a month or so of searching and asking around I finally got some answers which came from David Morris of DMRH (this guy is a wizz when it comes to Rotary knowledge)  he gave me one picture (show above) and the following information.

 

 This is a from the Mazda RX-9 book.

The origins of the RX-9 can be traced back to the 70's, when the RX-4 was known as the Luce in Japanese market.  When production of the RX-4 ceased in 1978 it's successor was also called the Luce (in the Japanese markets) only this time it was an ugly 4 door hardtop. The 78-79 models had ugly twin upright headlights but the 80-81 version looked almost exactly like a Mercedes of the day, hence it's nickname on the export market as the Mercedes version 929.

Mazda exported the car as a 929 with a piston engine. They fully intended at the time to export the same car as a rotary option as well. The rotary version was to be called the RX-9 as you would have been able to choose the piston 929 or the rotary RX-9.

What stopped them at the eleventh hour was the fact that the RX-5/Cosmo was a total failure as an export as it was too Japanese pretending to be American. Huge hit in Japan though.

So the logic was that the RX-9/Luce would suffer the same fate. With the RX-7 craze getting towards top gear, they simply didn't want to chance the car & ruin any possible RX-7 success.

Worth noting is the fact that Mazda brought about 6 into Australia for Evaluation as well. They still had the "Luce" badge but the RX-9 intension would have been there.

In the early 90's Mazda tried again with the RX-8. This was to be sold as a separate export & was actually the domestic version "Eunos Cosmo" with the famed 20B engine. Its export name was to be the RX-8. A friend of David Morris actually has a genuine RX-8 badge from the 90's (how COOL!) as it actually made it that far before Mazda again pulled the pin at the 11th hour. That was 1991.
