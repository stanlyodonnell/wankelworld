Secret Preview
     Mazda RX8 Secrete Preview Well after about the LONGEST! week and a half, the day finally arrived. On January 25th 2003 at 12:35pm I got my first look at the full production version of all new and much talked about Mazda RX-8. This was at the Yokohama (about 30 minutes from Tokyo) Mazda R&D center, I was an invited guest at the "Mazda RX-8 Secret Preview"

The event started of with a 20 minute slide show/video. The slide show began with a history of the sport in general then moved on to the history of Mazda and finally onto the history of the Rotary engine, mainly the Mazda Rotary. Next came the video this video was not new to me as I had seen it at the Tokyo Auto Saloon 15 days earlier (you can also see it here), but even so I still watched it with great interest.

After the video had finished and we had been told the rules and regulation for the day (there was to be absolutely no photography of any kind (I was so disappointed), but you could ask as many question as you wish, you could sit in, touch and admire the cars, accessories and shop options as much as you liked, with in the 80 minute time limit) we where led into the next display hall where the RX-8s were waiting.

As we walked into the hall all the Mazda staff that were waiting inside started clapping for us (I don't know why must be a Japanese thing, but it was kind of nice) and then showed us to our sits. The room was set out (click here for lay out) similar to a cafe with each person (or small group) being seated at a small round table with 4 chairs a round it. On each table there was sales guide folder (this is a smaller version of what the sales staff use to help answer any Q's you have) and a "Shop Option" brochure together with a price guide that had been flattened out and laminated separately. In the back left hand corner of the room was a drink area this is where the cute Mazda girls would get either tea, coffee or juice for you. And in the back right hand corner of the room was a long table pilled high with gifts (click here to see what I and everyone else received). On the front left hand side wall of the room was a table with all the "Shop Options" and on the front right hand side w there were 2 display boards the one closest to the front of the room showed all 8  paint colours that will be available, the next board showed the seat fabric/colour options that will be available. And the center focus of the room was of coarse the RX-8s. There were 3 THREE!! RX-8s on the left was the RX-8 Type S (Sports) in the middle was the RX-8 Type A (luxury) and finally on the right was the RX-8 Type E (base model).
