Felix Wankel

1902 - 1988

 

The inventor of the rotary engine

The Man Behind the Engine

Felix Heinrich Wankel was born on August 13, 1902, in Lahr, Germany. A brilliant but unconventional engineer, Wankel never attended university—his expertise came from natural genius and relentless curiosity.

The Dream

"I began to see that if you want to build a new type of engine, you must first build a new type of man."

Wankel's obsession was creating an engine without reciprocating parts. He believed the piston engine's up-and-down motion was inefficient and sought a design with purely rotational movement.

The Invention

In 1954, Wankel established the Felix Wankel Institute and began serious development of his rotary engine concept. The first working prototype, the DKM 54, ran on February 1, 1957.

The Mazda Connection

In 1961, Wankel signed a licensing agreement with NSU Motorenwerke. This agreement allowed other companies to develop the rotary engine—including Mazda. While other manufacturers experimented with the rotary and abandoned it due to challenges, Mazda saw potential and committed to solving its problems.

Legacy

Felix Wankel received numerous honors during his lifetime, including honorary doctorates from several universities. In 1986, Germany issued a postage stamp honoring Wankel and his engine—a rare tribute for an engineer. He died on October 9, 1988, at age 86, having lived to see his creation power everything from sports cars to aircraft.
