Mazda Rotary Models

Mazda Cosmo Sport (1967-1972)

The world's first production twin-rotor rotary engine car. The Cosmo Sport 110S was Mazda's bold statement to the world that the rotary engine was ready for the road.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 10A (982cc twin-rotor)
  • Power: 110 hp (Series I) / 128 hp (Series II)
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual / 5-speed manual
  • Production: 1,519 units

Legacy

The Cosmo Sport established Mazda as a serious sports car manufacturer. Its futuristic styling by Matasaburo Maeda and Heiji Ikeda made it an icon of 1960s automotive design.

R100 / Familia Rotary (1968-1973)

Mazda brought the rotary to the masses with the R100, a rotary-powered version of the Familia compact sedan and coupe.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 10A (982cc twin-rotor)
  • Power: 100 hp
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Body Styles: 2-door coupe, 4-door sedan

Legacy

The R100 proved the rotary could work in an affordable, everyday car. It was Mazda's first rotary export to the United States.

RX-2 / Capella Rotary (1970-1978)

The RX-2 combined the practicality of a mid-size sedan with the smooth power of the new 12A rotary engine.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 12A (1,146cc twin-rotor)
  • Power: 120 hp / 130 hp (later models)
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual / 5-speed manual
  • Body Styles: 2-door coupe, 4-door sedan

Legacy

The RX-2 won acclaim for its refined performance and helped establish the RX naming convention that would become legendary.

RX-3 / Savanna (1971-1978)

The RX-3 became Mazda's rotary-powered warrior in touring car racing, dominating the Japanese Grand Prix and establishing the rotary's motorsport credentials.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 10A / 12A (1,146cc twin-rotor)
  • Power: 100-130 hp
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual / 5-speed manual
  • Body Styles: 2-door coupe, 4-door sedan, wagon

Legacy

The RX-3 won the Japanese Grand Prix for seven consecutive years (1972-1978), cementing its place in motorsport history.

RX-4 / Luce Rotary (1972-1977)

Mazda's flagship rotary sedan combined luxury appointments with smooth rotary power, competing in the executive car segment.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 12A / 13A (1,308cc twin-rotor)
  • Power: 125-135 hp
  • Transmission: 5-speed manual / 3-speed automatic
  • Body Styles: 2-door coupe, 4-door sedan, wagon

RX-5 / Cosmo AP (1975-1981)

The second-generation Cosmo, also known as Cosmo AP (Anti-Pollution), featured an updated design and improved emissions.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 12A / 13B (1,308cc twin-rotor)
  • Power: 125-135 hp
  • Transmission: 5-speed manual / 3-speed automatic
  • Body Styles: 2-door hardtop

RX-7 SA/FB (1978-1985)

The original RX-7. Designed from the ground up as a pure sports car, the SA/FB established the formula: front mid-engine, rear-wheel drive, perfect weight distribution, and that unmistakable rotary character.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 12A / 12A Turbo / 13B
  • Power: 100-135 hp (12A) / 165 hp (Turbo)
  • Weight: ~1,000-1,100 kg
  • 0-60 mph: 9.2 seconds (12A) / 6.9 seconds (Turbo)

Legacy

The RX-7 saved Mazda during the oil crisis. Its efficient, compact rotary engine delivered sports car performance without the fuel consumption penalty of larger piston engines.

RX-7 FC (1986-1991)

The second-generation RX-7 brought modern styling, turbocharging, and refined handling. It competed directly with Porsche's 944 and 924.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 13B / 13B Turbo II
  • Power: 146 hp (NA) / 182-200 hp (Turbo)
  • Weight: ~1,200-1,300 kg
  • 0-60 mph: 7.4 seconds (NA) / 6.1 seconds (Turbo)

Legacy

The FC introduced the convertible variant and established the RX-7 as a serious performance car on the world stage.

RX-7 FD (1992-2002)

Arguably the finest rotary sports car ever made. The FD RX-7 is a masterpiece of design and engineering, with sequential twin-turbocharging delivering the broadest power band of any rotary engine.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 13B-REW Sequential Twin-Turbo
  • Power: 255 hp (1992-1995) / 276 hp (1996-2002)
  • Weight: ~1,280 kg
  • 0-60 mph: 5.1 seconds

Legacy

The FD is now a certified classic, with values rising steadily. Its timeless styling by Yoichi Sato and balanced handling make it one of the greatest Japanese sports cars ever created.

RX-8 (2003-2012)

The RX-8 redefined the rotary sports car with its unique 4-door, 4-seat configuration and the high-revving Renesis engine. No other car combined sports car handling with genuine practicality.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: Renesis 13B-MSP (6-port / 4-port)
  • Power: 189-238 hp
  • Redline: 9,000 rpm
  • Weight: ~1,300-1,380 kg

Legacy

The RX-8 was the last production rotary-powered car from Mazda. Its innovative suicide doors and practical packaging proved the rotary could do more than just power pure sports cars.

Concept Cars

Mazda has continued to develop rotary technology through concept vehicles, keeping the dream alive for enthusiasts worldwide.

Notable Concepts

  • RX-01 (1995): A compact rotary concept that hinted at a simpler, lighter future for the RX-7.
  • RX-EVOLV (2000): The concept that previewed the RX-8's unique 4-door design.
  • 16X (2007): Featured a larger 1.6L twin-rotor engine with direct injection.
  • RX-VISION (2015): A stunning front-engine, rear-drive rotary concept that reignited hopes for a new rotary sports car.
  • RX-VISION GT3 CONCEPT (2020): The racing version of the RX-VISION for Gran Turismo Sport.
