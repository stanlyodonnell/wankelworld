TAG Croco Amphibious Vehicle

The Croco

The Croco is articulated amphibious vehicle produced by TAG of Switzerland in 1983. The Croco also know as the Rhino. The TAG group was hoping that the military or oil and gas exploration companies would be interested in the Croco as it is small enough that it can be transported into remote locations via helicopters. 

A little more on the articulated section of the Croco. It is split into 2 sections (tubs) the front and back tubs, although attached, can rotate independently of each other in the vertical plane. Via a large roller bearing in the centre of the 2 sections. This means that one of the front wheels can go over something l rock or log, while the  rear wheel on the opposite can go over another large object at the same time (very cool) This is a truly amazing ability for a reasonably small vehicle.

Another amazing ability of the little wankel powered vehicle is that it has no problem with climbing a slope of 45degrees, or even a sideways slope of 40 degrees. All while carrying a payload of 500kg or 6 people. 

This is just a small sample of what the Croco can do. Photo courtesy  of Lane motor museum
The huge wheels and tyres on the Croco are constant 4x4 this help to avoid axel wind up. I believe that the rights of the vehicle are now with Kolper near Karlsruhe Germany.

Engine

Croco rotary engine (produced under NSU-Wankel licence), single rotor, displacement 440 cc, equivalent to 880 cc, compression ratio 7.6:1, oil-cooled rotor, engine air-cooled by centrifugal blower, fitted with electric as well as manual recoil starter.

Transmission

Rack and pinion steering with ball-jointed rods to all wheels. Detachable steering wheel.

Brakes

Mechanical, foot-operated braking system, dual circuit, single disc. Handbrake on gearbox. Brake effect by worm pinion differential transaxles.

Electrical system

12V, 500W alternator, battery capacity 66 Ah, capacitive discharge ignition, combined starter and main switch, 12V socket, front and rear position lights, high beam and dipped lights, flashing indicators and hazard warning lights, stop lights, number plate illumination, horn, windscreen wipers and washer.

Fuel system

Three standard 20 litre metal containers, fully interchangeable. Total capacity 60 litres. Standard grade petrol. Vacuum fuel pump. Special carburettor, petrol consumption 6 litres per hour.

Chassis and hull

Twin steel tubes, jointed at the pivoting point, sealed for amphibious operation, thoroughly rust-proofed and painted.
