Mazda MX-5 M2-1002

 

 
M2 was Mazda's relatively short lived department for concept cars that were built in very limited numbers. M2-CORP released it's second Roadster in late 1992, with a sightly different front bumper, it had all the same goodies as the previous 1001 Roadster, but offered a more luxurious and unique looking interior option, the Ivory Leather interior with wood accents like Brake handle and shifter. And was also limited to 300 units produced. This one did not do as well as the 1001, so the got stuck with some left over interiors. Which later were later installed as the Tokyo Limited, and only 40 of those made it to the streets of Japan.
