Rotary Engine History

 

The evolution of the Wankel rotary engine through the decades.

 

10A (1967-1973)

Mazda's first production rotary. 982cc twin-rotor powering the Cosmo Sport and R100.

12A (1970-1985)

Increased displacement to 1,146cc. Used in RX-2, RX-3, RX-4, RX-5, and early RX-7.

13B (1973-2012)

The most-produced rotary engine. 1,308cc twin-rotor powering everything from the RX-4 to the RX-8.

20B (1990-1996)

The only production three-rotor engine. 1,962cc in the Eunos Cosmo.

Renesis (2003-2012)

The final evolution with side exhaust ports. Powered the RX-8.

R26B (1991)

The legendary four-rotor racing engine that won Le Mans in the 787B.
