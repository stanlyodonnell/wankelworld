Classic Mazda Rotary Models

The early years of Mazda's rotary program produced some of the most striking automotive designs of the 1960s and 70s. From the futuristic Cosmo Sport to the racing-dominant RX-3, these cars established the rotary's reputation.

The Golden Era

Mazda's commitment to the rotary engine throughout the 1970s created a diverse lineup of vehicles. The company experimented with rotary power in everything from compact cars to large sedans, even developing a rotary-powered pickup truck (the REPU) and a rotary bus.

RX-7 Through the Generations

The RX-7 is the definitive rotary sports car. Three generations spanning 24 years, each with its own character and following.

SA/FB (1978-1985)

The original. Light, simple, and pure. The first-generation RX-7 established the formula that would define the model: front-mid engine, perfect weight distribution, and that unmistakable rotary sound.

FC (1986-1991)

The second generation brought modern styling and turbocharging. The FC competed directly with Porsche's 944 and established the RX-7 as a world-class sports car. The convertible variant added open-air rotary enjoyment.

FD (1992-2002)

Widely considered one of the finest Japanese sports cars ever made. The FD's timeless styling, sequential twin-turbo performance, and incredible handling make it a certified classic. Values continue to climb as enthusiasts recognize its significance.

Motorsport Heritage

The rotary engine's motorsport legacy is unmatched. From dominating Japanese touring car racing to winning the world's greatest endurance race, the rotary has proven itself on track.

Japanese Grand Prix Dominance

The Mazda RX-3 won the Japanese Grand Prix for an incredible seven consecutive years (1972-1978). This dominance established Mazda's reputation for building tough, competitive racing engines.

IMSA Racing

In North America, RX-7s were prolific in IMSA racing. The RX-7 holds the record for the most wins by any model in IMSA history. Teams loved the engine's reliability and the car's balanced handling.

Le Mans Victory

The pinnacle of rotary racing achievement. In 1991, the Mazda 787B with its screaming R26B four-rotor engine won the 24 Hours of Le Mans outright—the first Japanese manufacturer and only rotary-powered car to achieve this feat.

1991 Mazda 787B at Le Mans

The Sound

The 787B's sound is legendary. At full throttle, the R26B four-rotor engine produces a shriek unlike anything else in motorsport. It's been described as "an angry swarm of bees" or "a Formula 1 car from an alternate universe." The sound is so distinctive that fans can identify a 787B approaching from miles away.

Engine Bays

There's something beautiful about a well-maintained rotary engine bay. The compact engine leaves room for clean layouts, and the distinctive shape of the rotor housings makes every rotary engine instantly recognizable.

The Art of the Rotary

From the early 10A with its simple carburetor to the sequential twin-turbo 13B-REW with its complex vacuum system, each generation of rotary has its own character. Clean engine bays showcase the mechanical beauty of Felix Wankel's design.

Modified Rotaries

The aftermarket has embraced the rotary engine. From simple street builds to all-out racing engines, rotary modifications range from mild to wild. Bridge-ported 13Bs, peripheral port monsters, and turbocharged beasts—each has its place in rotary culture.

Events & Meets

The rotary community is passionate and active. From small local meets to massive international gatherings, rotary enthusiasts love to share their passion.

SevenStock

The largest annual rotary gathering in North America. Held in Southern California, SevenStock attracts hundreds of rotary cars and thousands of fans. It's the ultimate celebration of Mazda's rotary heritage.

Rotary Revival

Events around the world where rotary owners gather to share stories, parts, and knowledge. These meets are as much about community as they are about cars.

Track Days

Rotary engines love to rev, and track days let them stretch their legs. The sound of multiple RX-7s at full throttle on a race track is automotive music at its finest.
