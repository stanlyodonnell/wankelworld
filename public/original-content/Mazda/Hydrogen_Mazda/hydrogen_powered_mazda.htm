    We have all heard of the hydrogen powered Mazda RX-8 and I will be talking about this on another page very soon. But here on this page I will be discussing my a temps to run my 1997 Mazda Bravo Ute on hydrogen gas. I don't know about you but I am sick and tired of paying the highly inflated petrol price, not only that but the damage that we are doing to this planet is unforgivable. So I decide I should do all I can to try and make a difference. After searching the net for a while I found a few plans for so called on demand hydrogen generators, this means I won't have to store the hydrogen gas on board. Sure sound good to me BUT do these things really work. Well that is what I intend to find out.

    Firstly I will give a little back ground on the car that I am experimenting on. This is a 1997 Mazda Bravo Dual Fuel Ute (Factory fitted LPG and normal petrol). This car has approximately 165,000 km on the clock. Apart from a new exhaust it is still how Mazda made it back in July 1997.

     I will post up all plans that I obtain, so that even if I can't it get my Ute to run on hydrogen, maybe someone else will get their car to run on hydrogen. And pass their tips on to me and everyone else.

    The first set of plans that I found was sent into a magazine called Spirit of Ma'at and this was the opening line. "These plans were sent to the Spirit of Ma'at anonymously, from someone who does not want his or her name printed (for obvious reasons)." Now as you can well image if someone has truly found the key to producing hydrogen on demand in an automobile, there would be a lot of very powerful people wanting to keep this from getting out to the public, isn't that right Mr. Bush?
