Mazda Car Reviews

Mazda Millenia 2000

First it was called the Eunos 800, then it became known as the Mazda Eunos 800 and finally the Mazda Millenia.

When released it was available in one (automatic) version only and was a luxury car with plenty of innovative features. The power source is Mazda Millenia 2.3 litre V6 Miller-cycle engine that, with its Lysholm compressor (supercharger) and shortened compression stroke, produces more torque than a conventional 3.0 litre engine in the same class.


Mazda continues to equip its flagship with four-wheel steering, which apart from giving the car a tighter turning circle, is claimed to provide crisp and precise driver control. Traction control is another standard feature.

Other features of the Millenia include front driver, passenger and side airbags, anti-lock brakes, automatic air conditioning, power-operated glass sunroof, alloy wheels, cruise control, power-operated windows, exterior mirrors and door locks, vehicle speed-sensitive power steering, electrically adjustable front seats and leather upholstery.

The sound system is a high quality nine-speaker Bose unit with radio, cassette and an in-dash six CD player. Its a neat and compact system thats easy to operate.

Improvements to the suspension, steering and brakes have improved the driving feel, while extra body strengthening has benefited both handling and noise levels.

As with the previous models, quality standards are very high in the Millenia.

However, Mazda still persists in not providing a rubber wear mat under the drivers heel and protective mats will be needed to avoid carpet damage or premature wear.

Though it lacks the stretch-out space, the Millenia can readily accommodate four adults of average size. The front seats are generally comfortable but they lack shaping for good lateral support when cornering briskly. Rear outer occupants are also comfortably seated, but a centre rear occupant is perched high, with little seat padding and a large floor hump to contend with.

Considering that the Millenia's engine is only 2.3 litres in capacity and that the car weighs just over 1600 kg, performance is quite good. Sure, there are times when more low-down power would be welcome, however on an overall basis, the performance is lively enough and the engine remains delightfully smooth throughout its operating range.

The value of four-wheel steering may be debatable when it comes to ultimate handling capabilities, but it certainly does provide a tighter turning circle than would normally be possible in a car of this size. The Millenia owners handbook carries a caution to be careful when entering or exiting a parking space, as 4WS causes the rear of the car to move sideways slightly. 

So in summary the Mazda Millenia was a more appealing proposition than the outgoing Eunos 800.

All round, the Millenia rates as a very good luxury vehicle thats certainly worthy of consideration; the difficulty for Mazda is that there are plenty of opposition makes in this segment wearing badges with a higher prestige value.

One last thing I would like to add is make sure you check that the vehicle always takes premium unleaded fuel.
